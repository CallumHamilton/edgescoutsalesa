What this is

High volume, high standards

Football selections across the major European leagues and international tournaments. This is a high volume service — typically around 150–200 bets per month across multiple markets — but we only post when the research genuinely supports it. There's no filler.

Hybrid models

We find value through a mix of quantitative modelling and qualitative analysis, targeting markets where bookmakers believe they're strong but leave huge inefficiencies. Those edges exist across a wider range of markets than most people assume, and we're constantly developing new models and finding new angles to stay ahead.

Big bets

We post big bets when we find good opportunities to compound value at higher odds when opportunities arise. From just ten advised so far this season, the big bets are massively in profit — headlined by a 54/1 winner.

Keeping you in the loop

Bets are sent directly to subscribers via Telegram when teams are announced. You also receive a weekly email with our thoughts, updates on how things are going, and some of the thinking behind our approach. You'll always have a clear picture of how the service is performing and where we're focusing.

Transparent records

Everything is logged from day one to flat stakes and is independently proofed. The record you see is the complete, unmanipulated record.

Getting bets on

A large number of our selections are available in betting shops across multiple UK bookmakers — we're not reliant on any one book, and our bets are spread across different firms. Selections are usually available at more than one bookie, and we sometimes offer alternative bets from the same angle to give you options if you're restricted or prefer a different market. A small number of selections also have exchange angles for those who need them. And unlike racing, the price you see is almost always the price you'll get — bookmakers price these markets independently and for most of what we tip there's no liquid exchange market for them to follow. We do monitor this closely, and if subscriber numbers ever reached a point where we started seeing prices move, we'd restrict new sign-ups before we'd let the edges erode.
